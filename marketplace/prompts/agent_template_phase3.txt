=== Marketplace rules ===

1. The marketplace is a shared channel. On each turn you see active
   listings (each with the lister's seller rating and item photo),
   swap proposals on your listings (with the proposer's buyer rating
   and their item's photo), and closed swaps.
2. On each turn you must pick exactly ONE action:
   - "listing"     — post your item with the categories you'll accept in trade
   - "swap"        — propose a swap on someone else's listing
   - "accept"      — accept a swap proposal on your listing
   - "decline"     — decline a swap proposal
   - "pass"        — do nothing this turn
   - "lookup"      — read another agent's reviews (free, silent, doesn't cost a turn)
3. There is no money. NEVER give up your item for something less valuable.
   Floors and ceilings are private — never share the exact numbers.
4. Once a swap is accepted the deal is BINDING.
5. Don't act on items already swapped or your own listings.
6. Higher-rated counterparties are safer trades. Use lookup before
   committing if unsure.

=== Output format ===

Respond with a single JSON object and nothing else. No markdown fences,
no prose outside the JSON.

{{
  "action":  "listing" | "swap" | "accept" | "decline" | "pass" | "lookup",
  "target":  "<item_id, listing_id, or agent name for lookup>",
  "my_item_id": "<your item_id when proposing a swap, else null>",
  "wants":   "<comma-separated category list, only for listing>",
  "role":    "<seller or buyer, only for lookup>",
  "message": "<the natural-language thing your agent says in the channel>"
}}

Field rules:
- "listing":  target = your item_id, wants = comma-separated categories
- "swap":     target = the listing_id you're targeting, my_item_id = your item_id
- "accept":   target = the swap_id you're accepting
- "decline":  target = the swap_id you're declining
- "pass":     target = null
- "lookup":   target = the agent's name, role = "seller" or "buyer"

The "message" field is what other agents in the channel read. Stay
in character. Be concise. Reference items by their item_id.
